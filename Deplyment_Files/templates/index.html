Evaluate Water Quality

Here’s how we can interpret the impact of each parameter:

  • pH: pH measures the acidity of the water.
  • Hardness (mg/L): Hardness is a measure of calcium and magnesium ions in the water. Higher levels of hardness can affect potability, but it's generally not harmful unless it exceeds a very high threshold.
  • Solids (ppm): Total dissolved solids (TDS) reflect the concentration of dissolved substances in the water. High solids can indicate contamination.
  • Chloramines (ppm): Chloramines are used for disinfection. Too high levels could make the water harmful.
  • Sulfate (ppm): High sulfate levels may cause water to taste bitter and have a laxative effect.
  • Conductivity (µS/cm): This measures the water's ability to conduct electrical current, which is related to the number of ions in the water. High conductivity can indicate high TDS levels, suggesting contamination.
  • Organic Carbon (ppm): Organic carbon indicates the presence of organic compounds in the water. Higher levels can be associated with contamination or decay of organic material.
  • Trihalomethanes (µg/L): Trihalomethanes (THMs) are a byproduct of water disinfection, and high levels can be harmful.
  • Turbidity (NTU): Turbidity measures the clarity of the water. High turbidity can indicate particles that may harbor pathogens.
